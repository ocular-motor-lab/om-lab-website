1. Saccades

Saccade kinematics and internal signal cascade. Tests main sequence (amplitude–velocity), oblique trajectory linearity, double-step refractoriness, and the full Robinson cascade.

Saccade Main Sequence

Peak velocity vs amplitude scatter (left) and aligned eye traces (right) for amplitudes 0.5–20°.

Expected behavior All data within ±20% of 700(1−e^{−A/7}). Peak ≈660 deg/s at 20°.

📖 Bahill et al. (1975) Science; Robinson (1975) J Neurophysiol

Oblique Saccades

Sequence of saccades to 5 oblique targets. Left: 2D trajectory. Center/right: H and V components.

Expected behavior Straight 2D trajectories. H and V components synchronised (end together).

📖 Smit et al. (1990) J Neurophysiol

Saccade Double-Step Refractoriness

Double-step paradigm: target jumps 0→A/2 then A/2→A for A=10,20,30,40° after variable ISI (20–500 ms). Target shown dashed in same colour as eye trace. Short ISIs produce one amended saccade; longer ISIs produce two.

Expected behavior ISI < ~100 ms: 1 saccade (merged). ISI > ~150 ms: 2 separate saccades. Refractory period roughly constant across amplitudes (~150 ms).

📖 Becker & Jürgens (1979) Vision Res

Saccade Signal Cascade (with noise)

Row-by-row signal flow for 1°, 5°, 20°, 40° saccades: position, visual cascade + hold, accumulator (z_acc) + trigger IBN (z_trig) + OPN (z_opn), residual error, burst, eye velocity.

Expected behavior e_held freezes at saccade onset; z_acc rises to threshold then drains to floor (−0.5) during burst; z_trig suppresses OPN; accumulator floor enforces ~270 ms ISI.

2. VOR / OKR

Vestibulo-ocular reflex and optokinetic response. Replicates Raphan et al. (1979) Fig.9: VOR in dark, OKN+OKAN, and VVOR. Tests post-rotatory and OKAN time constants, VVOR gain, and nystagmus waveform.

Raphan 1979 Fig. 9 Replication

Panels A–F matching Raphan et al. (1979) Fig.9. Left col: SPV only. Right col: CUP (canal estimate), INT (velocity storage), SPV overlaid. A/B: VOR in dark. C/D: OKN+OKAN. E/F: VVOR (light→dark).

Expected behavior A: post-rot SPV TC 10–30 s. C: OKN gain~1, OKAN TC~20 s. E: VVOR gain>0.85 during rotation; post-rot TC similar to A. B/D/F: INT follows SPV; CUP decays at canal TC (~5 s).

📖 Raphan, Matsuo & Cohen (1979) Exp Brain Res 35:229–248

behavior

VOR / OKR Signal Cascade (Internal)

Left column (VOR in dark): head velocity → canal afferents → VS state → NI state → eye velocity. Right column (OKN): scene velocity → delayed retinal slip → VS → NI → eye velocity.

Expected behavior VOR: canal signal decays (TC ~5 s), VS extends it. OKR: slip_delayed drives VS and NI until eye velocity matches scene.

3. Gravity Estimator

Canal-otolith interaction: OCR, OVAR, VOR tilt suppression, OCR vs tilt angle, somatogravic OCR frequency dependence. Parameters: tau_grav=5.0, K_gd=2.864788978927615, g_ocr=1.019367991845056 (Laurens & Angelaki 2011).

Ocular Counterroll (OCR)

Counter-roll traces for tilts [5.0, 10.0, 20.0, 30.0, 45.0, 60.0, 75.0, 90.0]° + SS scatter. g_ocr=1.02.

Expected behavior Torsion ≈ −G_OCR×G0×sin(θ). 30° → ≈−5.0°. g_est holds flat after tilt (no drift).

📖 Boff, Kaufman & Thomas (1986); Laurens & Angelaki (2011)

behavior

OCR cascade (4-condition comparison)

30.0° tilt — VS, NI torsion cascade + SPV for scene+target / target only / scene only / dark.

Expected behavior Torsion stabilises at ≈-4.95°. SPV row: -VS (dashed) and -canal FT (dotted) should bracket the measured SPV. Yaw/pitch near 0 (pure roll tilt).

OVAR — Off-Vertical Axis Rotation

60°/s rotation, tilt angles [10.0, 30.0, 60.0, 90.0]°. Replicates Laurens & Angelaki (2011) Fig 5.

Expected behavior SPV sinusoidally modulated at rotation period. Modulation amplitude ∝ sin(tilt). g_est oscillates ±G₀ sin(α).

📖 Laurens & Angelaki (2011) Exp Brain Res 210:407

behavior

VOR Tilt Suppression

Upright 60°/s yaw; tilt [0.0, 30.0, 60.0, 90.0]° applied after stop. Replicates Laurens & Angelaki (2011) Fig 6.

Expected behavior Per-rotatory identical. TC decreases with post-stop tilt. 0°: TC ≈ τ_vs. 90°: TC ≈ τ_canal.

📖 Laurens & Angelaki (2011) Exp Brain Res

behavior

Somatogravic OCR — Frequency Dependence

Sinusoidal lateral translation at [0.03, 0.07, 0.15, 0.3, 0.7, 1.5] Hz, 2.0 m/s² peak. OCR via gravity estimator LP filter.

Expected behavior Corner frequency fc = K_grav/(2π) ≈ 0.032 Hz. OCR amplitude ∝ 1/√(1+(f/fc)²). Low f → peak ≈ 2.04°; high f → near 0°.

📖 Mayne (1974); Laurens & Angelaki (2011)

4. Smooth Pursuit

Smooth pursuit and catch-up saccades. Tests velocity gain at multiple speeds, sinusoidal tracking, and the pursuit integrator + saccade interaction cascade.

Smooth Pursuit — Velocity Range

Step-ramp target at 5, 10, 20, 40 deg/s. Blue: smooth pursuit enabled. Gray: saccades only (pursuit off). Rows: position, velocity, pursuit drive (total + integrator + phasic).

Expected behavior At 5–10 deg/s: pursuit tracks closely, steady-state gain > 0.8. At higher velocities: catch-up saccades + partial pursuit.

📖 Lisberger & Westbrook (1985) J Neurosci; Rashbass (1961)

behavior

Sinusoidal Pursuit (H + V)

Horizontal sinusoidal target at 0.2, 0.5, 1.0 Hz (peak 15 deg/s). Vertical at 2× horizontal frequency for Lissajous trajectory. Rows: H position, V position, 2D trajectory.

Expected behavior Low freq (0.2 Hz): near-unity gain, small phase lag. High freq (1.0 Hz): gain < 1, larger lag, catch-up saccades.

📖 Lisberger et al. (1981) J Neurophysiol

behavior

Smooth Pursuit Signal Cascade (Internal)

Full signal chain for 20 deg/s pursuit: target ramps 0.2–2.0 s then holds. Pursuit integrator persists after target stop (τ ≈ 40 s). Rows: position, velocity, pursuit drive (total/integrator/phasic), visual error, saccade accumulator, burst, refractory state.

Expected behavior Phasic drive rises fast at onset, decays to 0 at steady state. After target stops at 2 s: phasic reverses (retinal slip now backward), integrator decays slowly — eye coasts then corrects with catch-up saccades. Integrator TC visible as slow drift in x_p post-stop.

📖 Lisberger & Westbrook (1985)

5. Vergence

Binocular vergence eye movements driven by disparity. Symmetric convergence isolates vergence dynamics. Asymmetric vergence combines a version saccade with a depth change. Fixation disparity benchmark measures residual vergence error vs. target distance.

Vergence: Symmetric + Asymmetric, Both Directions

3 rows × 4 cols. Cols: symmetric conv, symmetric div, asymmetric right+conv, asymmetric left+div. Row 0: L/R eye yaw. Row 1: vergence (L−R). Row 2: version (L+R)/2.

Expected behavior Symmetric: L/R yaw diverge/converge equally, version ≈ 0. Asymmetric: both eyes saccade then vergence separates them. Rise TC ≈ 0.5–1 s. Version and vergence components are disconjugate.

📖 Mays (1984) J Neurophysiol; Collewijn et al. (1988) J Physiol; Zee et al. (1992) J Neurophysiol

behavior

Fixation Disparity vs. Distance

Steady-state vergence residual error at eight target distances (0.2–3 m). Left: vergence time-courses (near = warm colours). Right: fixation disparity in arcmin — the residual after fusion settles. Positive = under-convergence (exo FD). Green band = normal ±6 arcmin.

Expected behavior Fixation disparity <10 arcmin across the physiological range (Ogle 1964: 0–6 arcmin). Near targets may show slightly larger exo FD due to reduced fusional gain. Vergence TC ~0.5–2 s.

📖 Ogle, Martens & Dyer (1967); Semmlow & Hung (1983) Vision Research 23(3)

behavior

Diplopia: Fusion Gate and Vergence Limits

3 rows × 2 cols. Col 0: fused (3→0.3 m midline). Col 1: diplopic (3→0.05 m midline). Both columns use default params (saccades on). Row 0: L/R eye yaw; fused shows geometric target lines (±geo/2), diplopic shows NPC motor-limit lines (±NPC/2) with off-scale annotation. Row 1: vergence (L−R) with geometric target and NPC limit. Row 2: version (L+R)/2 — auto-scaled; diplopic case shows version shift from monocular fixation saccade driven by dominant eye retinal error.

Expected behavior Fused: L/R eye yaw converges to ±geo/2 dashed targets; vergence reaches geo target; version stays ≈ 0. Diplopic: monocular fixation saccade fires (dominant R eye drives both eyes); R eye lands on its geometric target (−geo/2); L eye deviates. Vergence limited well below geometric target.

📖 Fender & Julesz (1967) J Opt Soc Am; Schor (1979) Vision Res; Shipley & Rawlings (1970) Vision Res

Asymmetric Vergence Debug Cascade

7 rows × 2 cols. Left: asym right+conv (2→0.5 m, +10°). Right: asym left+div (0.5→2 m, −10°). Rows: eye position, vergence (L−R), version (L+R)/2, vergence velocity, OPN gate z_act, internal states e_held/x_copy/x_verg [H], Zee burst [H].

Expected behavior OPN gate: pulses during version saccade; Zee burst fires during the same window and exhausts as x_copy reaches e_held. Vergence velocity: overshoot appears as negative region after peak. x_verg[H] settles at geometric vergence target.

6. Fixation

Fixational eye movements driven by different noise sources. Tests canal noise filtering, retinal position OU drift (microsaccades), and retinal velocity noise (pursuit-like drift).

Fixational Eye Movements — Noise Source Comparison

5-column comparison: noiseless, canal noise only, retinal position OU drift, retinal velocity noise, and all three combined. Rows: eye position, velocity, saccade burst, noise signal.

Expected behavior Noiseless: eye stays at 0°. Canal noise: slow drift + rare microsaccades. Pos noise: OU drift → corrective microsaccades when error exceeds threshold. Vel noise: smooth pursuit-like drift.

📖 Rolfs (2009) Neurosci Biobehav Rev 33:1597–1627

behavior

7. Listing's Law

Torsional constraint T = OCR − H·V·π/360: saccade plane scatter and OCR-driven correction saccade.

Listing's Plane

Saccades to a (H, V) grid; torsion measured at steady state. g_ocr=0 so OCR=0 and T_required = −H·V·π/360.

Expected behavior Scatter points lie on identity line; cross-sections linear with slope −V·π/360.

📖 Tweed, Haslwanter & Fetter (1998) IOVS; van Rijn & van den Berg (1993) EBR

behavior

OCR Torsional Correction Saccade

Head tilt 30°; g_ocr=1.02. Torsional listing error drives a corrective saccade.

Expected behavior With saccades: torsion jumps to ≈-5.0° after accumulator delay (~80 ms × n_sacs). Without saccades: slow exponential rise with τ≈τ_i=25 s.

📖 Listing (1854); Tweed et al. (1998)

behavior

Listing's Plane During Smooth Pursuit

Sinusoidal H pursuit (15° at 0.5 Hz) at V=20°. vel_torsion demand added to NI keeps torsion on Listing's plane continuously.

Expected behavior Eye torsion tracks T_required = −H·20·π/360 in real time; Listing error RMS < 0.5°.

📖 Tweed, Haslwanter & Fetter (1998) IOVS; van Rijn & van den Berg (1993) EBR
