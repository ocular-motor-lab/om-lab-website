3. Tilt / Translation

Otolith-driven eye movements: OCR, OVAR, and VOR tilt suppression. OCR uses g_ocr=0.13 (13% physiological gain). OVAR and tilt suppression use K_gd=0.5 (gravity dumping). NOTE: gravity estimator not yet validated.

Ocular Counterroll (OCR)

Head rolls 30° and holds. Otolith → gravity estimator → torsional OCR via g_ocr=0.13.

Expected behavior OCR ≈ 3.9° counter-rolling. Settling TC ≈ 2 s (1/K_grav). g_est_y → G₀ sin(30°) ≈ 4.9 m/s².

📖 Boff, Kaufman & Thomas (1986); Tweed et al. (1994)

behavior

OVAR — Off-Vertical Axis Rotation

Constant 60°/s rotation around axis tilted 20° from vertical. Canal signals constant velocity; otolith sees sinusoidal gravity direction. K_gd=0.5 links gravity estimate to VS dumping.

Expected behavior Sinusoidal SPV modulation at period = 6 s. g_est y oscillates between ±3.4 m/s². NOTE: gravity estimator not yet validated.

📖 Raphan et al. (1981); Angelaki & Hess (1994) J Neurophysiol

behavior

VOR Tilt Suppression

Upright vs. 90° roll-tilted yaw VOR decay (K_gd=0.5, 20 s rotation at 60°/s). Gravity dumping selectively drains VS for motion perpendicular to gravity.

Expected behavior τ_upright ≈ 20–35 s (VS extends canal TC). τ_tilted: shortened by gravity dumping (τ_eff = τ_vs/(1+K_gd·τ_vs)). NOTE: gravity estimator not yet validated.

📖 Raphan et al. (1981) J Neurophysiol; Cohen et al. (1983)

5. Vergence

Binocular vergence eye movements driven by disparity. Symmetric convergence isolates vergence dynamics. Asymmetric vergence combines a version saccade with a depth change.

Symmetric Vergence (Convergence)

Both eyes converge symmetrically when a near target appears at midline. Saccades disabled to isolate vergence dynamics.

Expected behavior Δvergence ≈ 11.0° (far→near; IPD=6 mm). Version ≈ 0. Rise TC: fast phasic component + slow integration (τ ≈ 400 ms within Panum's area).

📖 Mays (1984) J Neurophysiol; Cumming & Judge (1986) J Physiol

behavior

Asymmetric Vergence

Target moves 10° rightward AND closer (2 m → 0.5 m). Version (conjugate) and vergence (disconjugate) should separate.

Expected behavior Version ≈ 10° (saccade). Vergence ≈ 7.3° (near). Components independent (saccade TC ~ 50–70 ms; vergence TC ~ 400 ms).

📖 Collewijn et al. (1988) J Physiol; Zee et al. (1992)

behavior

7. Efference Copy

Tests that self-generated motor commands (saccades, pursuit) do not contaminate velocity storage or pursuit integrator via retinal slip. EC-corrected slip ≈ 0 during saccades; VS stays flat in stationary scene.

EC — Saccade in Lit Scene

20° saccade in stationary lit scene. Row-by-row: eye velocity, burst command, raw retinal slip (spikes during saccade), motor EC (delayed opposing signal), corrected slip (raw + EC), VS net + pursuit state (both should stay near 0).

Expected behavior slip_raw spikes during burst. motor_ec provides equal/opposite delayed correction. slip_corr ≈ 0. VS net and pursuit state remain flat (< 1 deg/s) post-saccade.
