3. Gravity Estimator

Otolith-driven eye movements via the cross-product gravity transport model. g_est tracks head-frame gravity direction; OCR is driven by g_est[1] (lateral gravity component) via g_ocr=0.13. K_gd=0.5 links g_est to velocity-storage dumping for OVAR and tilt suppression.

Ocular Counterroll (OCR)

Step-ramp head rolls to 5°–60°, then hold 10 s. Row 1: eye torsion time traces for all angles (dotted = expected G_OCR·G₀·sin θ). Row 2: g_est[1] settling (0 = upright). Row 3: SS torsion vs tilt angle scatter with sine prediction.

Expected behavior OCR ≈ G_OCR·G₀·sin(θ). 30° → ≈3.9°. g_est settles to −G₀·sin(θ) and holds flat (no drift).

📖 Boff, Kaufman & Thomas (1986); Laurens & Angelaki (2011)

behavior

OVAR — Off-Vertical Axis Rotation

Constant 60°/s rotation around axis tilted 10°, 30°, or 60° from vertical. Canal signals constant velocity; otolith sees sinusoidal gravity direction cycling at the rotation rate.

Expected behavior SPV sinusoidally modulated at rotation period. g_est[1] oscillates ±G₀·sin(α). Modulation amplitude ∝ sin(tilt).

📖 Raphan et al. (1981); Angelaki & Hess (1994) J Neurophysiol

behavior

VOR Tilt Suppression

Post-rotatory SPV decay for 0° (upright), 30°, 60°, 90° pre-tilt (K_gd=0.5, 20 s yaw at 60°/s). Gravity dumping selectively shortens VS time constant perpendicular to gravity. Inset: TC vs tilt angle.

Expected behavior 0° (upright) τ ≈ τ_vs. TC decreases monotonically with tilt. 90°: τ_eff = τ_vs/(1+K_gd·τ_vs).

📖 Raphan et al. (1981) J Neurophysiol; Cohen et al. (1983)

Somatogravic OCR Frequency Dependence

Sinusoidal lateral translation (2 m/s² peak) at 0.03–1.5 Hz. Low-pass gravity estimator converts GIA → perceived tilt → OCR. Bode plot: peak torsion vs frequency with LP theory overlay.

📖 Mayne (1974); Laurens & Angelaki (2011)

4. Listing's Law

Half-angle rule enforcement: torsion T satisfies T = OCR − (H−H₀)(V−V₀)·π/360 in rotation-vector space. Saccades auto-correct torsional error toward Listing's plane; pursuit adds torsional velocity demand proportional to H·dV/dt + V·dH/dt.

Listing's Plane

Saccades to a 5×5 grid of targets (±15° H, ±15° V). Left: 3D scatter of eye rotation vectors after each saccade. Right: torsion vs predicted half-angle value (identity line = perfect Listing's law).

Expected behavior All points within ±0.3° of the Listing's plane (T ≈ −H·V·π/360 for primary position at origin). Scatter plot lies on identity line.

📖 Tweed & Vilis (1987) J Neurophysiol; Haslwanter (1995)

behavior

Listing's Law + OCR Interaction

Head rolls 20° (OCR tilts Listing's plane), then saccades to eccentric targets. Tests that saccade torsion correction uses OCR-shifted primary position.

Expected behavior After head roll, g_ocr offsets primary torsion. Saccades to oblique targets still land on the (tilted) Listing's plane. Torsion error < 0.5°.

📖 Haslwanter et al. (1992); Crawford & Vilis (1991)

behavior

Listing's Law During Pursuit

Sinusoidal horizontal pursuit (±15°, 0.5 Hz) at constant vertical offset (20°). The half-angle rule demands torsional velocity T̈ = −π/360·(H·V̇ + V·Ḣ), which the NI must track.

Expected behavior Torsion tracks T_expected = −H·V·π/360 with RMS error < 0.5°. Torsion oscillates at pursuit frequency.

📖 Tweed et al. (1994) J Neurophysiol; Misslisch et al. (1994)

6. Vergence

Binocular vergence eye movements driven by disparity. Symmetric convergence isolates vergence dynamics. Asymmetric vergence combines a version saccade with a depth change.

Symmetric Vergence (Convergence)

Both eyes converge symmetrically when a near target appears at midline. Saccades disabled to isolate vergence dynamics.

Expected behavior Δvergence ≈ 11.0° (far→near; IPD=6 mm). Version ≈ 0. Rise TC: fast phasic component + slow integration (τ ≈ 400 ms within Panum's area).

📖 Mays (1984) J Neurophysiol; Cumming & Judge (1986) J Physiol

behavior

Asymmetric Vergence

Target moves 10° rightward AND closer (2 m → 0.5 m). Version (conjugate) and vergence (disconjugate) should separate.

Expected behavior Version ≈ 10° (saccade). Vergence ≈ 7.3° (near). Components independent (saccade TC ~ 50–70 ms; vergence TC ~ 400 ms).

📖 Collewijn et al. (1988) J Physiol; Zee et al. (1992)

behavior
