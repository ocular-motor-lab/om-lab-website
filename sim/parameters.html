OculomotorJax — Parameter Reference

All learnable and fixed parameters across SensoryParams, PlantParams, and BrainParams. Defaults are healthy adult values; pathology columns show how to perturb them.

Semicircular Canals  SensoryParams

Steinhausen torsion-pendulum model (Fernandez & Goldberg 1971). Second-order bandpass: cupula adaptation (τ_c) cascaded with endolymph inertia (τ_s). Six canals; push-pull pairs LARP / RALP / horizontal.

Parameter Default Units Description & physiology Anatomical locus Pathology / perturbation
tau_c 5.0 s Cupula adaptation time constant. High-pass corner ≈ 1/(2π·τ_c) ≈ 0.03 Hz. Sets the low-frequency rolloff of the VOR.
Fernandez & Goldberg (1971) J Neurophysiol 34:635
 Cristae ampullaris; cupula mechanics
UVH: unchanged (peripheral)
tau_s 0.005 s Endolymph inertia time constant. Low-pass corner ≈ 32 Hz. Anatomically fixed; effectively makes canals ideal integrators in the physiological frequency range. Semicircular canal lumen (fluid inertia; anatomically fixed)
Fixed (anatomical)
canal_gains ones(6) — Per-canal sensitivity scale. 1 = intact; 0 = complete paresis. Scales only the modulation above/below resting discharge — the resting discharge (floor) is unaffected, so reducing gain models reduced hair-cell transduction without creating a spurious resting bias. Hair cells, cristae ampullaris; type I/II afferents
UVH: [:3] = 0.1 Labyrinthectomy: [:3] = 0
canal_floor 80.0 deg/s Canal resting discharge at rest (x₂ = 0). Also the inhibitory saturation point (Ewald's 2nd law). Surviving afferents in UVH maintain this resting rate.
Goldberg & Fernandez (1971) J Neurophysiol 34:635
 Vestibular nerve (CN VIII); primary afferents

Otolith  SensoryParams

First-order low-pass adaptation (Fernandez & Goldberg 1976). Passes sustained gravity but adapts out prolonged linear acceleration (GIA → gravity estimate).

 Utricular / saccular macula; otoconia
Otolith hypofunction

Visual Pathway  SensoryParams

40-stage gamma cascade per eye (scene_vel, target_pos, target_vel, scene_visible, target_visible). Mean delay = tau_vis; group delay ≈ 80 ms.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionAnatomical locusPathology
tau_vis 0.08 s Mean visual delay (γ-cascade). Cascade has 40 stages; τ_stage = tau_vis / 40 = 2 ms. Must satisfy dt < 2·τ_stage (dt ≤ 4 ms with defaults).
Lisberger & Movshon (1999) J Neurosci 19:1262
 Retina → LGN → V1 → MT/MST; total visual latency
visual_field_limit 90.0 deg Monocular visual field half-width. Target beyond this eccentricity sets target_in_vf → 0 → SG enters quick-phase (centering) mode. Retina (peripheral limit); V1 retinotopic map
Hemianopia: 45° Tunnel vision: <30°
k_visual_field 1.0 1/deg Sigmoid steepness of the visual field gate. Larger → sharper boundary.

Sensory Noise  SensoryParams

All noise sources are zero by default (deterministic). Pre-generated before diffeqsolve and injected via LinearInterpolation — ODE remains differentiable. sigma_pos uses an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process to generate microsaccade-inducing drift.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionAnatomical locusEffect
sigma_canal 0.0 deg/s Canal afferent noise SD. Filtered heavily by VS/NI/plant. Realistic ~1–3 deg/s drives low-frequency nystagmus-like drift. Vestibular nerve discharge variability
Fixation drift; VOR noise
sigma_slip 0.0 deg/s Retinal slip noise SD. Drives VS/OKR even without head or scene motion. Retinal ganglion cell noise; MT/MST variability
Spurious OKR
sigma_pos 0.0 deg Retinal position noise SD. OU process accumulates slowly, crossing the SG threshold occasionally to generate sparse corrective microsaccades. White noise would fire SG continuously. Foveal fixation instability; SC fixation neurons
Microsaccades
tau_pos_drift 0.3 s OU drift time constant for retinal position noise. Controls inter-microsaccade interval: larger τ → slower drift → longer intervals.
sigma_vel 0.0 deg/s Target velocity noise SD. Drives pursuit integrator. MT/MST velocity signal variability
Pursuit jitter

Velocity Storage  BrainParams

Bilateral push-pull VN architecture (Raphan et al. 1979). Two populations (x_A, x_B) with net = x_A − x_B feeding NI. Stores head-velocity signal beyond canal TC via positive feedback. Null adaptation (x_null) shifts effective equilibrium for extended OKAN / PAN modelling.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionAnatomical locusPathology
tau_vs 20.0 s Velocity storage / OKAN time constant. Extends effective VOR TC from τ_c (5 s) to ~15–20 s via positive feedback.
Cohen et al. (1977) J Neurophysiol 40:532
 Medial vestibular nucleus (MVN); nodulus/uvula (cerebellum)
UVH: ~10 s VN infarct: ~5 s Cerebellar: normal or ↑
tau_vs_pitch_frac 0.4 — Pitch VS TC as fraction of tau_vs (→ 8 s). Shorter than yaw; driven by Raphan 1979, Dai 1991, Angelaki 1995. SVN (anterior canal-driven) / IVN (posterior canal-driven)
tau_vs_roll_frac 0.15 — Roll VS TC as fraction of tau_vs (→ 3 s). Much shorter; fixes torsion overshoot in OCR benchmarks. MVN torsional cells; interstitial nucleus of Cajal (INC)
b_vs 100.0 deg/s VN resting bias. Scalar broadcasts to all 6 pop states; pass a (6,) array for asymmetry. Sets both the equilibrium firing rate and the input responsiveness of each population (via g_pop = b_vs / B_NOMINAL scaling). Asymmetry → spontaneous nystagmus. Vestibular nucleus (VN) tonic discharge; type I/II neurons
Acute UVH: lesioned side 70 VN infarct: lesioned side 0 Chronic: → 100 (compensated)
tau_vs_adapt 600.0 s VS null-adaptation time constant. x_null slowly tracks VS output; effective equilibrium shifts → extends TC during sustained stimulation or drives slow PAN oscillation. Nodulus/uvula (cerebellar) → MVN; long-term VS plasticity
Nodulus/uvula lesion: → ∞ (prolonged OKAN) PAN: ~30–60 s

OKR / OKAN  BrainParams

Optokinetic pathways: direct (g_vis, fast onset) and integrating (K_vis → VS, slow OKAN buildup). Scene-gated and EC-corrected. OKN steady-state gain ≈ (2·K_vis·τ_vs + g_vis)/(1 + 2·K_vis·τ_vs + g_vis).

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionAnatomical locusPathology
K_vis 0.1 1/s Visual-to-VS integration gain (OKR/OKAN charging). Effective bilateral gain = 2·K_vis.
Raphan et al. (1979) J Neurophysiol 42:1109
 NOT/AOS → MVN integrating pathway
NOT lesion: → 0 Hemianopia: reduced
g_vis 0.6 — Direct visual feedthrough gain. Provides fast OKR onset before VS charges. OKR inner loop: L(jω) ≈ g_vis·exp(−jω·τ_vis) — must be <1 for stability (g_vis=1 → zero gain margin → ~6 Hz onset ringing).
Raphan et al. (1979) Fig. 8: gl = 0.6
 NOT/AOS direct (phasic) pathway to VN
v_max_okr 80.0 deg/s NOT/AOS velocity saturation. OKR gain ≈ 1 below 30 deg/s, half-max ~60 deg/s, near-zero ~100 deg/s.
Hoffmann (1979) Exp Brain Res; Cohen et al. (1977)
 NOT/AOS velocity-tuned neurons; cortical MT/MST input

Bilateral push-pull NPH/INC (Robinson 1975). Net = x_L − x_R. Null adaptation (x_null) shifts the leak target for rebound nystagmus modelling. Anti-windup at ±orbital_limit.

Cannon & Robinson (1985) J Neurophysiol 54:1332

Gaze-evoked nystagmus: ~5 s Cerebellar (FL/PFL): 3–8 s Alexander's law: asymmetric

Mismatch → glissade
tau_vis (EC copy) 0.08 s EC cascade delay copy. Must match SensoryParams.tau_vis for burst-cancel cancellation to hold in VS/pursuit. Mismatch → saccadic contamination of OKAN / pursuit. Efference copy pathway; SC → cerebellum → VN
b_ni 0.0 deg NPH intrinsic resting bias. 0 = no net drift at straight-ahead. Set >0 for unilateral NPH lesion modelling. NPH / INC resting discharge balance

Saccade Generator  BrainParams

Robinson (1975) local-feedback burst model with bilateral EBN/IBN populations. Signal flow: retinal error (e_held, pre-loaded by SC) → rise-to-bound accumulator (z_acc) → trigger IBN (z_trig) → OPN pause → EBN/IBN burst → motor command + x_copy decrement. ISI set by accumulator floor: ISI ≈ (threshold_acc − acc_burst_floor) × τ_acc ≈ 270 ms.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionAnatomical locusPathology
Burst ceiling & main sequence
g_burst 700.0 deg/s EBN/IBN burst ceiling (deg/s). Peak eye velocity saturates at g_burst·(1−e^{−A/e_sat_sac}).
Bahill, Clark & Stark (1975) Math Biosci 26:303
 Excitatory burst neurons (EBN): PPRF (horizontal); riMLF (vertical/torsional)
Slow saccades (CPEO, SCA): ↓ Cerebellar hypometria: ↓
e_sat_sac 7.0 deg Main-sequence amplitude saturation constant. Controls slope of peak-velocity vs. amplitude curve. SC motor map → PPRF amplitude encoding
EBN / IBN population dynamics
tau_bn 0.003 s EBN/IBN membrane TC. States track the error drive (e_held) with lag. Heun stability: (1 + g_opn_bn·100)·dt/tau_bn < 2 → must keep tau_bn > 2.5 ms at dt=1 ms. PPRF (horizontal EBN/IBN); riMLF (vertical/torsional)
g_opn_bn 0.04 1/fr OPN→BN multiplicative suppression gain. Shortens BN TC to tau_bn/(1+g_opn_bn·act_opn) ≈ 0.6 ms when OPN=100 (fixation); effectively clamps BNs at rest. During burst (act_opn=0): TC = tau_bn = 3 ms. Heun limit: g_opn_bn < 0.09. OPN collaterals → EBN/IBN
g_opn_bn_hold 0.4 1/fr OPN→BN additive inhibitory offset. At tonic OPN (=100): adds 40° of inhibition, keeping BN equilibrium at (e_held−40)/5 ≈ −8° → IBN silent for errors up to ~40°. Zero during burst (act_opn=0), freeing BNs to fire. OPN → PPRF IBN inhibitory synapses
g_ibn_bn 0.143 — IBN→BN contralateral inhibition gain (= g_ci/g_burst = 100/700). Element-wise per axis: L IBN → R EBN/IBN and vice versa. Provides directional selectivity without separate direction circuits. IBN → contralateral PPRF/riMLF
OPN — omnipause neurons
tau_sac 0.001 s OPN membrane TC. Governs how fast z_opn drops to 0 (burst on) and recovers to 100 (burst off). Recovery TC = tau_sac / k_tonic_opn ≈ 2 ms. Omnipause neurons (OPN); nucleus raphe interpositus (RIP)
k_tonic_opn 0.5 — OPN tonic recovery gain. Always active; pulls z_opn → 100 with TC = tau_sac/k_tonic = 2 ms. Latch condition: IBN inhibition must exceed tonic recovery, i.e. g_ibn_opn·IBN_total > k_tonic·100 = 50. OPN intrinsic depolarization; collicular excitation
g_opn_pause 500.0 fr z_trig-driven OPN inhibitory overshoot. z_trig drives z_opn membrane below spike threshold by up to g_opn_pause units; act_opn = relu(z_opn) clips at 0. Large value → OPN pauses in ~2 ms after z_acc crosses threshold.
Yoshida et al. (1999); Scudder et al. (2002)
 Trigger IBN → OPN (pre-burst suppression)
↓ → slower OPN pause → hypometric main sequence
g_ibn_opn 200.0 fr/s IBN→OPN Schmitt-trigger latch gain. Once IBN fires, g_ibn_opn·IBN_total >> k_tonic·z_opn → OPN stays suppressed throughout the burst. At burst end: e_held→0 → IBN→0 → OPN recovers. Suppression condition (g_ibn_opn=200 > 50=k_tonic·100): always satisfied once burst latches. IBN → OPN (Schmitt-trigger latch)
↓ → OPN breakthrough → truncated burst (hypometria) ↓↓ → flutter / oscillations
Trigger IBN (z_trig) — pre-burst OPN suppression
tau_trig 0.002 s Trigger IBN rise TC. z_trig charges from charge_sac (relu of z_acc − threshold_acc) and is drained by IBN. Provides smooth, delayed OPN suppression onset — decouples accumulator crossing from instantaneous OPN drop. Heun stability: (1+g_ibn_trig)·dt/tau_trig < 2 → must keep tau_trig > 1.5 ms. Trigger IBN population (long-lead burst neuron analog)
g_ibn_trig 2.0 — IBN→z_trig drain gain (ibn_norm ∈ [0,1]). Once the main IBN burst fires, it rapidly drains z_trig — TC during burst ≈ tau_trig/(1+g_ibn_trig) ≈ 0.7 ms. This releases OPN when the burst ends, preventing re-trigger. IBN → trigger IBN (feedback inhibition)
Rise-to-bound accumulator (z_acc)
tau_acc 0.180 s Accumulator rise TC. Noise-robust: brief retinal error spikes (<τ_acc) cannot reach threshold. ISI ≈ (threshold_acc − acc_burst_floor)·τ_acc = 1.5·180 ms = 270 ms. Controls saccade latency after target appears. SC buildup neurons; caudate nucleus (BG gate)
↓ → shorter latency, less noise robustness
threshold_acc 1.0 — Accumulator trigger threshold. charge_sac = relu(k_acc·(z_acc − threshold_acc)) → zero below threshold (no pre-trigger OPN leak), ramps to 1 within 2 ms above it. SC buildup → OPN disinhibition threshold
k_acc 500.0 — Accumulator→z_trig relu gain. Sets how sharply charge_sac ramps above threshold_acc. With k_acc=500 and z_acc 0.002 above threshold, charge_sac already reaches 1 — effectively a hard threshold at dt=1 ms resolution. SC buildup → trigger IBN projection
acc_burst_floor −0.5 — Accumulator target level during burst. IBN drains z_acc toward this floor (negative) while OPN is suppressed. Must re-climb the full distance (threshold_acc − acc_burst_floor = 1.5) to the next threshold crossing, setting the minimum ISI. IBN → SC buildup neuron inhibition
↑ toward 0 → shorter ISI → double-saccade risk in pursuit
tau_burst_drain 0.002 s Accumulator drain TC during burst. Drives z_acc → acc_burst_floor with this TC while ibn_norm > 0. Fast (2 ms) so that z_acc reaches the floor well before the burst ends for all amplitudes. Heun bound: g_acc_drain·dt/tau_burst_drain < 2. IBN → SC buildup inhibitory synapse (fast)
g_acc_drain 3.0 — IBN drain multiplier for z_acc. Boosts drain for small saccades (weak IBN, ibn_norm ≈ 0.024 for 1°) without breaking triggering for large saccades. Within Heun bound: g_acc_drain·dt/tau_burst_drain = 1.5 < 2. IBN → SC buildup inhibitory gain
tau_acc_leak 5.0 s Accumulator passive leak TC. Prevents subthreshold drift accumulation between saccades. Effect on ISI: ~5% at 5 s (negligible). Keep ≥ 5 s: faster values starve small saccades (0.5°, gate_err=0.5) during OPN suppression phase. SC buildup neuron passive membrane leak
sigma_acc 0.2 1/√s Accumulator diffusion noise SD. Adds trial-to-trial RT variability (±15–25 ms SD at 0.2). Set to 0 for deterministic (noiseless) simulations. SC / FEF decision noise
RT variability
Trigger gates & sample-and-hold
threshold_sac 0.5 deg Retinal error trigger threshold. gate_err = σ(k_sac·(|e| − threshold_sac)) gates the accumulator. Below this, z_acc does not charge (no saccade initiated). Rostral SC fixation zone; basal ganglia gating (SNr → SC)
↑ → hypometric / absent small saccades ↓ → oscillations / SWJ
threshold_sac_qp 2.0 deg Error threshold for quick-phase accumulation (target_visible ≈ 0). Higher value suppresses spurious centripetal resets during small-amplitude slow phases. Rostral SC; BG gate (SNr disinhibition); quick-phase trigger
↓ → more frequent quick phases
threshold_stop 0.1 deg Burst-stop threshold. Burst ends when |e_held − x_copy| < threshold_stop (residual error gone). PPRF local feedback; cerebellar vermis (lobule VII) stop signal
k_sac 200.0 1/deg Sigmoid steepness for gate_err (trigger) and burst-stop gates. Large value → effectively a step function. SC buildup / fixation neuron threshold sharpness
tau_hold 0.020 s Sample-and-hold tracking TC. e_held tracks current target between saccades (normalized_opn = 1); tracking frozen during burst (normalized_opn → 0). 180 ms accumulation / 20 ms TC = 9 TCs → 99.99% capture before latch. SC fixation neurons → SG hold register

Target Selection & Quick-Phase Generator  BrainParams

Internal to the saccade generator. When target_visible ≈ 1: standard saccade to retinal error clipped to orbital range. When target_visible ≈ 0: quick-phase generator fires centripetal resetting saccades (VOR nystagmus, spontaneous nystagmus) with predictive look-ahead to compensate for ongoing slow-phase drift.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionAnatomical locusPathology
orbital_limit 50.0 deg Oculomotor range half-width used by SG for e_target clipping. Should match PlantParams.orbital_limit. Orbital anatomy; EOM length–tension limits
alpha_reset 1.25 — Quick-phase centering gain. e_center = −α·x_ni_pred. 1 = full recentering; >1 = slight overshoot toward center (reduces half-cycle drift accumulation); <1 = partial reset (residual eccentricity). Nodulus/uvula → VN; quick-phase amplitude control
k_center_vel 0.75 — Quick-phase prediction gain. look_ahead = k·τ_ref; x_ni_pred = x_ni − look_ahead·w_vs_tonic. Prediction only in the quick-phase (out-of-field) path; voluntary saccades are unaffected. 0 = no prediction; 1 = full τ_ref look-ahead. SC; PPRF predictive signals; cerebellar timing

Vergence  BrainParams

Three-state binocular model: Fused (|disp_H| < panum_h) → fine fusional servo; Fusable (within motor limits) → coarse proximal drive; Diplopic (total demand beyond NPC or div_max) → vergence suppressed, dominant eye drives version. Parameters split into sensory limits (relative disparity space) and motor limits (absolute vergence angle). Vergence saccade pulse (Zee 1992) accelerates convergence/divergence during conjugate saccades. Tonic vergence sets the dark vergence = geometric demand at 1 m; phoria is a clinical measurement, not a model parameter.
