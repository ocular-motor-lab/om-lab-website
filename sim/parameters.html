OculomotorJax — Parameter Reference

All learnable and fixed parameters across SensoryParams, PlantParams, and BrainParams. Defaults are healthy adult values; pathology columns show how to perturb them.

Semicircular Canals  SensoryParams

Steinhausen torsion-pendulum model (Fernandez & Goldberg 1971). Second-order bandpass: cupula adaptation (τ_c) cascaded with endolymph inertia (τ_s). Six canals; push-pull pairs LARP / RALP / horizontal.

Parameter Default Units Description & physiology Pathology / perturbation
tau_c 5.0 s Cupula adaptation time constant. High-pass corner ≈ 1/(2π·τ_c) ≈ 0.03 Hz. Sets the low-frequency rolloff of the VOR.
Fernandez & Goldberg (1971) J Neurophysiol 34:635
UVH: unchanged (peripheral)
tau_s 0.005 s Endolymph inertia time constant. Low-pass corner ≈ 32 Hz. Anatomically fixed; effectively makes canals ideal integrators in the physiological frequency range.
Fixed (anatomical)
canal_gains ones(6) — Per-canal sensitivity scale. 1 = intact; 0 = complete paresis. Scales only the modulation above/below resting discharge — the resting discharge (floor) is unaffected, so reducing gain models reduced hair-cell transduction without creating a spurious resting bias.
UVH: [:3] = 0.1 Labyrinthectomy: [:3] = 0
canal_floor 80.0 deg/s Canal resting discharge at rest (x₂ = 0). Also the inhibitory saturation point (Ewald's 2nd law). Surviving afferents in UVH maintain this resting rate.
Goldberg & Fernandez (1971) J Neurophysiol 34:635

Otolith  SensoryParams

First-order low-pass adaptation (Fernandez & Goldberg 1976). Passes sustained gravity but adapts out prolonged linear acceleration (GIA → gravity estimate).

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
tau_oto 100.0 s Otolith adaptation time constant. Large value → near-DC pass; small value → heavy high-pass (adapts out sustained tilt).
Fernandez & Goldberg (1976) J Neurophysiol 39:985
Otolith hypofunction

Visual Pathway  SensoryParams

40-stage gamma cascade per eye (scene_vel, target_pos, target_vel, scene_visible, target_visible). Mean delay = tau_vis; group delay ≈ 80 ms.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
tau_vis 0.08 s Mean visual delay (γ-cascade). Cascade has 40 stages; τ_stage = tau_vis / 40 = 2 ms. Must satisfy dt < 2·τ_stage (dt ≤ 4 ms with defaults).
Lisberger & Movshon (1999) J Neurosci 19:1262
visual_field_limit 90.0 deg Monocular visual field half-width. Target beyond this eccentricity sets target_in_vf → 0 → SG enters quick-phase (centering) mode.
Hemianopia: 45° Tunnel vision: <30°
k_visual_field 1.0 1/deg Sigmoid steepness of the visual field gate. Larger → sharper boundary.

Sensory Noise  SensoryParams

All noise sources are zero by default (deterministic). Pre-generated before diffeqsolve and injected via LinearInterpolation — ODE remains differentiable. sigma_pos uses an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process to generate microsaccade-inducing drift.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionEffect
sigma_canal 0.0 deg/s Canal afferent noise SD. Filtered heavily by VS/NI/plant. Realistic ~1–3 deg/s drives low-frequency nystagmus-like drift.
Fixation drift; VOR noise
sigma_slip 0.0 deg/s Retinal slip noise SD. Drives VS/OKR even without head or scene motion.
Spurious OKR
sigma_pos 0.0 deg Retinal position noise SD. OU process accumulates slowly, crossing the SG threshold occasionally to generate sparse corrective microsaccades. White noise would fire SG continuously.
Microsaccades
tau_pos_drift 0.3 s OU drift time constant for retinal position noise. Controls inter-microsaccade interval: larger τ → slower drift → longer intervals.
sigma_vel 0.0 deg/s Target velocity noise SD. Drives pursuit integrator.
Pursuit jitter

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
ipd 0.064 m Inter-pupillary distance (~64 mm adult). Sets vergence geometry; scales disparity signal amplitude with viewing distance.

Orbital Mechanics  PlantParams

First-order Robinson (1964) plant: dx_p/dt = −x_p/τ_p + motor_cmd/τ_p. Eye position = motor_cmd at DC. tau_p must match BrainParams.tau_p (internal model copy in NI feedthrough).

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
tau_p 0.15 s Plant time constant (viscoelastic orbital mechanics). Mismatch with BrainParams.tau_p → post-saccadic glissade.
Robinson (1981); Goldstein (1983) Biol Cybern
Thyroid eye disease: ↑ Post-orbital surgery
orbital_limit 50.0 deg Mechanical half-range of the orbit. Anatomical constraint.
Duane syndrome: asymmetric Orbital fracture: ↓
k_orbital 1.0 1/deg Sigmoid steepness of the orbital mechanical limit.

Velocity Storage  BrainParams

Bilateral push-pull VN architecture (Raphan et al. 1979). Two populations (x_L, x_R) with net = x_L − x_R feeding NI. Stores head-velocity signal beyond canal TC via positive feedback. Null adaptation (x_null) shifts effective equilibrium for extended OKAN / PAN modelling.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
tau_vs 20.0 s Velocity storage / OKAN time constant. Extends effective VOR TC from τ_c (5 s) to ~15–20 s via positive feedback.
Cohen et al. (1977) J Neurophysiol 40:532
UVH: ~10 s VN infarct: ~5 s Cerebellar: normal or ↑
b_vs 100.0 deg/s VN resting bias. Scalar broadcasts to all 6 pop states; pass a (6,) array for asymmetry. Sets both the equilibrium firing rate and the input responsiveness of each population (via g_pop = b_vs / B_NOMINAL scaling). Asymmetry → spontaneous nystagmus.
Acute UVH: lesioned side 70 VN infarct: lesioned side 0 Chronic: → 100 (compensated)
tau_vs_adapt 600.0 s VS null-adaptation time constant. x_null slowly tracks VS output; effective equilibrium shifts → extends TC during sustained stimulation or drives slow PAN oscillation.
Nodulus/uvula lesion: → ∞ (prolonged OKAN) PAN: ~30–60 s

VOR Gain  BrainParams

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
g_vor 1.0 — VOR central gain. Scales canal bypass D-feedthrough to VS output. 1 = healthy; <1 = cerebellar adaptation down (e.g. magnifying lenses); >1 = adaptation up. Distinct from canal_gains (peripheral transduction sensitivity).
Cerebellar adaptation: 0.5–2.0 Post-UVH substitution: ↑
v_max_vor 400.0 deg/s Excitatory canal afferent saturation for VS drive. Clip applied before VS integration. Inhibitory saturation (~80 deg/s = canal_floor) is handled in canal.nonlinearity.
Goldberg & Fernández (1971) J Neurophysiol 34:635
K_vs 0.1 1/s Canal-to-VS integration gain. Effective bilateral gain = 2·K_vs = 0.2 (push-pull doubles net drive). Controls VOR charging speed.

OKR / OKAN  BrainParams

Optokinetic pathways: direct (g_vis, fast onset) and integrating (K_vis → VS, slow OKAN buildup). Scene-gated and EC-corrected. OKN steady-state gain ≈ (2·K_vis·τ_vs + g_vis)/(1 + 2·K_vis·τ_vs + g_vis).

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
K_vis 0.1 1/s Visual-to-VS integration gain (OKR/OKAN charging). Effective bilateral gain = 2·K_vis.
Raphan et al. (1979) J Neurophysiol 42:1109
NOT lesion: → 0 Hemianopia: reduced
g_vis 0.6 — Direct visual feedthrough gain. Provides fast OKR onset before VS charges. OKR inner loop: L(jω) ≈ g_vis·exp(−jω·τ_vis) — must be <1 for stability (g_vis=1 → zero gain margin → ~6 Hz onset ringing).
Raphan et al. (1979) Fig. 8: gl = 0.6
v_max_okr 80.0 deg/s NOT/AOS velocity saturation. OKR gain ≈ 1 below 30 deg/s, half-max ~60 deg/s, near-zero ~100 deg/s.
Hoffmann (1979) Exp Brain Res; Cohen et al. (1977)

Neural Integrator  BrainParams

Bilateral push-pull NPH/INC (Robinson 1975). Net = x_L − x_R. Null adaptation (x_null) shifts the leak target for rebound nystagmus modelling. Anti-windup at ±orbital_limit.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
tau_i 25.0 s NI leak time constant. Healthy NPH >20 s (quasi-perfect integrator). Shorter TC → eye drifts back toward straight-ahead → gaze-evoked nystagmus.
Cannon & Robinson (1985) J Neurophysiol 54:1332
Gaze-evoked nystagmus: ~5 s Cerebellar (FL/PFL): 3–8 s Alexander's law: asymmetric
tau_p (NI copy) 0.15 s Plant TC copy in NI feedthrough (pulse-step: u_p = x_ni + τ_p·u_vel). Should match PlantParams.tau_p. Mismatch → post-saccadic glissade or overshoot.
Mismatch → glissade
tau_vis (EC copy) 0.08 s EC cascade delay copy. Must match SensoryParams.tau_vis for burst-cancel cancellation to hold in VS/pursuit. Mismatch → saccadic contamination of OKAN / pursuit.
b_ni 0.0 deg NPH intrinsic resting bias. 0 = no net drift at straight-ahead. Set >0 for unilateral NPH lesion modelling.
Unilateral NPH lesion: asymmetric
tau_ni_adapt 20.0 s NI null-adaptation TC. Null slowly tracks net position; return to centre drives rebound nystagmus. τ → ∞ disables rebound.
Zee et al. (1980) J Neurophysiol 44:187
Rebound nystagmus: 10–30 s Bruns nystagmus: asymmetric

Saccade Generator  BrainParams

Robinson (1975) local-feedback burst model. Sample-and-hold (e_held) makes saccades ballistic; OPN refractory enforces inter-saccadic interval; rise-to-bound accumulator provides noise robustness and settling time before latch.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
Burst dynamics
g_burst 700.0 deg/s Burst ceiling. Peak velocity saturates at g_burst·(1−e^{−A/e_sat_sac}). Reduces peak velocity for large amplitudes (main sequence).
Bahill, Clark & Stark (1975) Math Biosci 26:303
Slow saccades (CPEO, SCA): ↓ Cerebellar hypometria: ↓
e_sat_sac 7.0 deg Main-sequence amplitude saturation constant. Controls the slope of the peak-velocity vs amplitude curve. Larger → steeper velocity scaling at small amplitudes.
Trigger gates
threshold_sac 0.5 deg Retinal error trigger threshold. gate_err = σ(k_sac·(|e| − threshold_sac)). Below this, no saccade is initiated.
Hypometric saccades: ↑ Oscillations / SWJ: ↓
threshold_stop 0.1 deg Burst-stop threshold. Burst ends when |e_held − x_copy| < threshold_stop (residual gone).
k_sac 200.0 1/deg Sigmoid steepness for trigger and stop gates. Large value → effectively a step function.
OPN refractory
tau_ref 0.15 s OPN refractory decay TC. Inter-saccadic interval ≈ τ_ref · ln(z_ref_peak / threshold_ref) ≈ 175 ms. Governs the minimum ISI.
Opsoclonus / flutter: ↓ (~30 ms) Square-wave jerks: ↓
tau_ref_charge 0.001 s OPN charge TC. Time to latch z_ref at peak (~1 ms). Should be much faster than tau_ref.
threshold_sac_release 0.4 — z_ref threshold to release OPN latch (z_opn → 100). Must be above threshold_ref to ensure proper sequencing.
k_ref 50.0 — Bistable OPN gate steepness. Large → hard switch at z_ref = threshold_ref.
threshold_ref 0.1 — OPN gate threshold. Below this, refractory is over and accumulator can charge again.
Sample-and-hold & accumulator
tau_hold 0.005 s Sample-and-hold tracking TC. e_held tracks current target between saccades; frozen during burst (z_idl² ≈ 0 when OPN off).
tau_sac 0.001 s Saccade OPN latch TC. Governs how fast z_opn drops to 0 (burst on) and returns to 100 (burst off).
tau_acc 0.080 s Accumulator rise TC. Ensures ~80 ms latency before saccade trigger. Noise-robust: brief retinal error spikes (< τ_acc) cannot cross threshold.
Reduced → shorter latency, less noise robustness
tau_drain 0.120 s Accumulator drain TC. When gate is off (target gone or refractory), z_acc decays toward 0. tau_drain > tau_acc ensures equilibrium z_acc > threshold for sustained targets.
threshold_acc 0.5 — Accumulator trigger threshold. Saccade fires when z_acc > threshold_acc (after ~τ_acc of sustained error).
k_acc 50.0 — Accumulator sigmoid steepness. Larger → sharper threshold crossing → more abrupt OPN pause.
tau_reset_fast 0.05 s Copy-integrator reset TC. Between saccades x_copy resets to 0; during burst with overshoot, x_copy springs toward e_held.

Target Selection & Quick-Phase Generator  BrainParams

Internal to the saccade generator. When target_visible ≈ 1: standard saccade to retinal error clipped to orbital range. When target_visible ≈ 0: quick-phase generator fires centripetal resetting saccades (VOR nystagmus, spontaneous nystagmus) with predictive look-ahead to compensate for ongoing slow-phase drift.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
orbital_limit 50.0 deg Oculomotor range half-width used by SG for e_target clipping. Should match PlantParams.orbital_limit.
alpha_reset 1.0 — Quick-phase centering gain. e_center = −α·x_ni_pred. 1 = full recentering; <1 = partial reset (residual eccentricity).
k_center_vel 0.5 — Quick-phase prediction gain. look_ahead = k·τ_ref; x_ni_pred = x_ni − look_ahead·w_vs_tonic. Prediction only in the quick-phase (out-of-field) path; voluntary saccades are unaffected. 0 = no prediction; 1 = full τ_ref look-ahead.

Gravity Estimator & OCR  BrainParams

Laurens–Angelaki (2011) model: Bayesian-like otolith–canal fusion. Cross-product transport rotates the gravity estimate as the head turns. Outputs gravity direction for OCR and somatogravic suppression.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
K_grav 0.6 1/s Otolith correction gain for gravity estimate. Controls how fast the internal gravity vector aligns with the otolith GIA.
Laurens & Angelaki (2011) J Neurosci 31:11414
K_lin 0.1 1/s Linear acceleration adaptation gain. Smaller → more somatogravic effect (brain attributes more linear acc. to gravity); larger → faster separation of gravity from linear acceleration.
Somatogravic illusion: K_lin → 0
K_gd 0.0 1/s Gravity dumping gain. Reduces VS net when GIA and g_est are misaligned (post-rotatory tilt). 0 = disabled.
g_ocr 0.0 deg Ocular counter-roll amplitude. Healthy ~10°; 0 = disabled (not yet validated). Adds a torsional offset to motor command proportional to lateral gravity component.
Otolith lesion: g_ocr → 0

Smooth Pursuit  BrainParams

Leaky integrator + Smith predictor (Lisberger 1988). Target-gated EC-corrected velocity drives integrator. Phasic feedthrough (K_phasic) provides fast onset; integrator stores steady-state velocity.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
K_pursuit 4.0 1/s Pursuit integration gain. Rise TC ≈ 1/K_pursuit ≈ 250 ms. Larger → faster pursuit initiation but more overshoot.
Cerebellar (FL): ↓ gain MST lesion: ipsilesional deficit
K_phasic_pursuit 5.0 — Pursuit direct feedthrough gain. Fast onset before integrator charges; reduces initial retinal error during ramp pursuit initiation.
tau_pursuit 40.0 s Pursuit leak TC. ~40 s → 97.5% gain at 1 Hz. Shorter → drift after target removed; larger → slower adaptation.
v_max_pursuit 40.0 deg/s MT/MST pursuit velocity saturation. Gain ≈ 1 up to ~30–40 deg/s, then falls.
Fuchs (1967) J Physiol 191:609; Lisberger & Westbrook (1985)

Vergence  BrainParams

Dual-range nonlinear drive: fusional (Panum's area, high gain) + proximal (full range, lower gain). Diplopia suppression gate: when disparity exceeds disp_max_verg_fus, dominant eye takes over version.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
K_verg 4.0 1/s Fusional vergence integration gain (within Panum's area). High gain for fine disparity correction.
Rashbass & Westheimer (1961) J Physiol 159:361
Convergence insufficiency: ↓
K_verg_prox 3.0 1/s Proximal vergence integration gain (full range). Lower gain for coarse range control.
K_phasic_verg 1.0 — Phasic feedthrough (applied only to fusional signal). Fast vergence onset on disparity step.
tau_verg 6.0 s Vergence leak TC. Leaks toward phoria. ~5–7 s (Semmlow 1986).
Exophoria: phoria drift
disp_max_verg_fus 2.0 deg Panum's fusional area half-width. Below: fused binocular vision; above: diplopia → suppression begins.
Strabismus: >2°
disp_max_verg_prox 20.0 deg Proximal vergence saturation. Full vergence range (~40° total).
Hung & Semmlow (1980)
eye_dominant 1.0 — 1.0 = right eye dominant, 0.0 = left. When diplopic, version saccades/pursuit driven by dominant eye only.
Amblyopia; monocular cover
phoria [0,0,0] deg Resting vergence (phoria). Tonic setpoint; vergence leaks toward this. phoria[0]>0 = esophoria (over-converged at rest), <0 = exophoria.
Intermittent exotropia: phoria[0] < −4°

Accommodation  BrainParams

Schor dual-interaction model (1979). Fast (phasic) + slow (tonic) lens adjustment. Cross-linked to vergence via AC/A and CA/C ratios.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionNote
tau_acc_fast 0.3 s Fast (phasic) lens TC. ~300 ms step response.
Hung & Semmlow (1980)
tau_acc_slow 30.0 s Slow tonic accommodation adaptation TC.
Schor (1979)
K_acc_fast 1.5 1/s Fast blur-to-accommodation gain.
K_acc_slow 0.3 1/s Slow integration gain.
AC_A 5.0 pd/D Accommodative convergence / accommodation ratio. At 40 cm (2.5 D): AC/A drives ~7.2° convergence. Explains why IXT decompensates preferentially at distance (low accommodation demand).
High AC/A: esotropia at near Low AC/A: exotropia
CA_C 0.4 D/pd Convergence accommodation / convergence ratio. Drives accommodation when vergence is disparity-driven; reduces defocus during vergence eye movements.

Motor Nucleus & Nerve  BrainParams

Two-stage encode: brain motor command → per-nucleus activation (M_NUCLEUS, 6×12) → per-nerve activation (M_NERVE_PROJ, 12×12). Nucleus order: ABN_L(0), ABN_R(1), CN4_L(2), CN4_R(3), CN3_MR_L/R(4–5), CN3_SR_L/R(6–7), CN3_IR_L/R(8–9), CN3_IO_L/R(10–11). Nerve order: LR, MR, SR, IR, SO, IO per eye.

ParameterDefaultUnits DescriptionPathology
g_nucleus ones(12) — (12,) per-nucleus gain [0, 1]. Zero = nucleus lesion (all muscles innervated by that nucleus affected).
ABN lesion: ipsilateral LR only CN4 nucleus: ipsilateral SO
g_nerve ones(12) — (12,) per-nerve gain [0, 1]. Zero = nerve/fascicular lesion (single muscle without affecting other nuclei).
CN3 palsy: MR/SR/IR/IO → 0 CN4 palsy: SO → 0 (hypertropia) CN6 palsy: LR → 0 (esotropia)
g_mlf_ver_L 1.0 — Version gain for left MR subnucleus of CN3 (via MLF, ABN_R→MR_L pathway). Zeroing = left INO: left eye cannot adduct on rightward gaze; vergence preserved.
Left INO: g_mlf_ver_L = 0 MS, stroke
g_mlf_ver_R 1.0 — Version gain for right MR subnucleus via MLF (ABN_L→MR_R). Zeroing = right INO.
Right INO: g_mlf_ver_R = 0
