Self Motion Estimation (18 fields)

Parameter Default Units Description Anatomical locus Disorders / notable values
tau_vs 20 s

Yaw velocity-storage time constant. Healthy ~20 s monkey, ~15 s human. Determines OKAN decay TC after scene off and post-rotational nystagmus decay in the dark.

Raphan, Matsuo & Cohen (1979) · Cohen, Henn, Raphan & Dennett (1981)
 MVN, nodulus/uvula Nodulus/uvula lesion: 1.5 Bilateral vestibular loss: 5.0
tau_vs_pitch_frac 1 —

Pitch VS TC = tau_vs × this fraction. Default 1.0 (yaw and pitch held equally well in the model). Literature suggests pitch may be slightly leakier than yaw (Hess & Dieringer 1991: ~5–10 s vs ~15–20 s yaw).

Hess & Dieringer (1991) · Angelaki & Henn (2000)
 MVN pitch axis
tau_vs_roll_fracneeds review 1 —

Roll VS TC = tau_vs × this fraction. Default 1.0; literature suggests roll storage is the leakiest axis (Dai et al. 1991: ~2–5 s). Consider lowering for closer literature match.

Dai et al. (1991) · Angelaki et al. (1995)
 MVN roll axis
b_vs 100 deg/s

VN resting firing rate (deg/s equivalent). Scalar broadcasts to all 6 states; pass a (6,) array for asymmetric (lesion) cases. Simultaneously sets equilibrium firing rate AND input responsiveness (canal drive scales by b_vs / B_NOMINAL = 100).

 Vestibular nucleus resting bias (Type-I VN tonic firing) Vestibular neuritis (one side): 70 VN infarct (one side): 0
tau_vs_adapt 600 s

VS null-adaptation TC. Default 600 s → negligible in normal demos. Reduce to ~30–60 s to engage PAN-like (periodic alternating nystagmus) slow oscillation through anti-symmetric VS drift.

 Cerebellar feedback to MVN (PAN-related) PAN: 45.0
g_vor 1 —

Central VOR gain — scales canal feedthrough to VS output. 1.0 = healthy; <1 = hypofunction / VOR adaptation down; >1 = adaptation up. Distinct from peripheral canal_gains (transduction sensitivity).

 Cerebellar VOR calibration (flocculus) VOR hypofunction: 0.5 VOR gain-up adaptation (BVH): 1.5
v_max_vor 400 deg/s

Excitatory canal afferent saturation. Inhibitory floor (~80 deg/s, Ewald's 2nd law) is implemented in canal nonlinearity. Excitatory ceiling 300–600 deg/s (Goldberg & Fernandez 1971).

Goldberg & Fernández (1971) J Neurophysiol 34:635
 Type-I VN saturation
K_vs 0.1 1/s

Canal-to-VS integration gain. Bilateral push-pull, so effective net gain = 2·K_vs.

Raphan et al. (1979)
 Canal afferents → MVN integrator input
K_vis 0.1 1/s

Visual-to-VS gain, drives OKR/OKAN charging. Bilateral push-pull, so effective net gain = 2·K_vis. Combined with g_vis, OKN steady-state gain ≈ 0.82 at K_vis=0.1, g_vis=0.6 (Raphan 1979).

Raphan (1979)
 NOT/AOS → MVN (visual-vestibular convergence) OKR/OKAN abolished: 0.0
g_vis 0.6 —

Direct visual feedthrough gain (Raphan 1979 Fig 8: gl=0.6). OKR inner loop stable iff g_vis < 1. Larger → faster OKR onset but ringing risk.

Raphan (1979)
 Direct pretectal / NOT visual pathway
v_max_okr 80 deg/s

Visual slip saturation feeding VS. NOT neurons saturate ~80 deg/s (Hoffmann 1979). OKR gain ≈1 below 30 deg/s, half-max ~60 deg/s.

Hoffmann (1979) Exp Brain Res · Cohen, Matsuo & Raphan (1977)
 NOT/AOS neurons
K_grav 0.6 1/s

Somatogravic gain — pulls g_est toward the GIA residual. L&A 2011 calls this "g_o" and uses 0.6 (faster commitment to tilt percept). Our 0.2 keeps the somatogravic bandwidth around 0.032 Hz, matching slower psychophysical tilt-percept onset (Mayne 1974, Holly 1996). Higher K_grav → stronger / faster somatogravic illusion.

Laurens & Angelaki (2011) J Neurosci 31:11414 · Mayne (1974); Holly (1996)
 Nodulus/uvula → MVN (otolith-canal fusion)
K_lin 0.1 1/s

Linear-acceleration adaptation gain — competing share-claim on the GIA residual. Smaller K_lin → more somatogravic effect (gravity wins more of the residual). Larger → faster separation of true translation from tilt.

Laurens & Angelaki (2011)
 Nodulus/uvula; PIVC Strong somatogravic illusion: 0.0
tau_a_lin 1.5 s

Decay TC of the linear-acceleration estimate. Bayesian "translation duration prior" — real self-motion accelerations are brief, so â returns to 0 in absence of sustained evidence. SS â for sustained residual r: K_lin · τ_a_lin · r.

Laurens, Meng & Angelaki (2013) PLoS Comp Bio
 Nodulus/uvula; deterministic stand-in for Bayesian translation prior
K_gd 2.86 —

Gravity-dumping gain. Scales the rotational feedback rf = (GIA × −g_est)/G0² fed into VS. Drives velocity-storage dumping ⊥ gravity (tilt suppression) and OVAR sustained nystagmus. Default 2.86 = L&A's 0.05 rad/s² in deg-converted units.

Laurens & Angelaki (2011) · Raphan & Cohen (1988)
 Nodulus/uvula → MVN (velocity-storage gravity dumping) Gravity-blind VS: 0.0
g_ocr 1.019 deg/(m/s²)

Ocular counter-roll gain — torsion command per unit lateral gravity. Default 10°/9.81 ≈ 1.019 → ~10° SS torsion at 90° lateral tilt.

Howard & Templeton (1966) · Diamond, Markham et al. (1979)
 Utricle → CN3 (IO/SR subnuclei); CN4 (SO) Utricle lesion: 0.0
tau_head 2 s

Linear-velocity integration TC. Longer τ_head → bigger v_lin during sustained translation (better DARK T-VOR gain) but more drift containment needed. Paige & Tomko (1991) DARK gain ~0.5–0.7 for 1.6 s pulses suggests τ_head in 2–4 s range; we use 10 s for stronger DARK T-VOR coupling.

Paige & Tomko (1991)
 Vestibular translational pathway (otolith integration)
K_he_vis 5 1/s

Visual-velocity pull gain on v_lin. Fuses scene translational flow with the vestibular integration of otolith. In dark the pull is toward 0, suppressing drift during pure rotation (OVAR, tilt suppression).

 MST translational flow → vestibular nuclei
